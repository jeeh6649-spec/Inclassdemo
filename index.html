A Short Introduction

Jeremy Ehly is a CU Boulder design professor and licensed architect whose teaching spans theory, design-build, digital representation, AI, and professional practice. His courses thrive on ambitious projects driven by intensely personal connections between students and their work.

Across ENVD, he asks students to unite rigorous thinking with meaningful doing: to research deeply, design responsibly, communicate with clarity, and understand the broader implications of what they make. His current teaching statement frames that work through ethics, human experience, ecological care, curiosity, rigor, and joy.

The result is a studio culture that is minimalist in form but expansive in possibility, equally comfortable with built work, visualization, fabrication, and the evolving questions surrounding AI in design education.
